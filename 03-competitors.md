# NeoPaaS — Direct Competitor Brief

13 US/EU startups, all funded 2022–2026, ranked by directness against NeoPaaS's three-dimensional pitch: **orchestration** (multi-acquirer routing), **reconciliation** (multi-branch settlement matching), and **regional compliance** (ZATCA e-invoicing).

The honest finding: **no single competitor does all three.** NeoPaaS's moat is the intersection. Below, each competitor is mapped to which dimension(s) they overlap with — and where NeoPaaS wins head-to-head.

---

## TIER 1 — Closest direct threats

These three are the ones to watch and the ones to position against in investor decks.

### 1. Yuno — **★★★★★**
- **URL:** [y.uno](https://www.y.uno) · **HQ:** New York, USA (regional office Doha, Qatar — opened Feb 2025)
- **What:** Global payment orchestration platform. Single API across acquirers, smart routing, retries, reconciliation.
- **Funding:** **$25M Series A, March 2024**, led by DST Global Partners (a16z, Tiger Global, Kaszek, Monashees); ~$150M valuation. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/03/05/yunos-payments-orchestration-valuation-fintech/)
- **Overlap:** Single-API orchestration across acquirers. **Already on the ground in the Gulf** — this is the only competitor with active MENA operations.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** ZATCA-native e-invoicing, pre-integrated Saudi acquirers (Mada, NeoLeap, Geidea), POS terminal fleet monitoring, MSP/POS-vendor distribution motion vs Yuno's enterprise-direct sales.

### 2. Payrails — **★★★★★**
- **URL:** [payrails.com](https://www.payrails.com) · **HQ:** Berlin, Germany
- **What:** Modular payments platform — orchestration, payouts, tokenization, automated reconciliation, **in-person payments**. Spun out of Delivery Hero.
- **Funding:** **$32M Series A, June 2025**, led by HV Capital Growth Fund (EQT Ventures, General Catalyst, a16z). [Payrails blog](https://www.payrails.com/blog/payrails-series-a)
- **Overlap:** Functional twin. Same product surface area — orchestration + reconciliation + in-person + multi-branch F&B DNA from the Delivery Hero origin story.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** ZATCA compliance built in, Saudi acquirer pre-integration, local POS-vendor channel vs Payrails' direct enterprise GTM.

### 3. allO — **★★★★★**
- **URL:** [allo.app](https://allo.app) · **HQ:** Berlin, Germany
- **What:** AI-native operating system for multi-branch restaurants. Consolidates POS, payments, delivery, and back-office into one dashboard.
- **Funding:** **$14M Series A, 2025**, led by Zigg Capital. [Hospitality Net](https://www.hospitalitynet.org/news/4132593/allo-raises-14m-series-a-led-by-zigg-capital-to-scale-the-first-ai-native-operating-system-for-restaurants)
- **Overlap:** Almost-identical pitch — *"owners who used to reconcile revenue across five separate systems now see everything in one dashboard,"* 1,000+ multi-branch locations live.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Card-acquirer-level settlement reconciliation and real-time ZATCA filing — allO is an ops layer, not a tax conduit or acquirer router.

## TIER 3 — Regional compliance analogs

The closest analogs for NeoPaaS's ZATCA play. These are not card-routing competitors — they're "tax authority pipe" companies in jurisdictions with similar mandates. They prove the regulatory-platform thesis is venture-fundable at scale.

### 8. Pennylane — **★★★★**
- **URL:** [pennylane.com](https://www.pennylane.com) · **HQ:** Paris, France
- **What:** French accounting/finance OS. **Certified PDP** for France's e-invoicing mandate (DGFIP submission). The clearest structural mirror of ZATCA Phase 2.
- **Funding:** **€175M Series E, January 2026**, led by TCV at $4.25B valuation. [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-20/tcv-blackstone-back-startup-pennylane-at-4-25-billion-value)
- **Overlap:** Same "platform-as-tax-conduit" model. French PDP architecture is the closest non-Saudi equivalent of ZATCA Phase 2.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Multi-branch merchant focus and acquirer reconciliation. Pennylane is accountant-centric and SMB-horizontal, not built for restaurants/retail with terminal fleets.

### 9. Fonoa — **★★★★**
- **URL:** [fonoa.com](https://www.fonoa.com) · **HQ:** Dublin, Ireland
- **What:** Global indirect-tax + e-invoicing API across 190+ jurisdictions (including ZATCA).
- **Funding:** **$110M Series C, May 2026**, led by Headline (Coatue, Dawn, Index, OMERS); concurrent acquisition of PwC Indirect Tax Edge. [BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260519379233/en/Fonoa-Acquires-PwCs-Tax-Platform-and-Raises-$110M-to-Build-Category-Defining-AI-Tax-System)
- **Overlap:** Real-time CTC/e-invoicing pipes. Directly analogous to NeoPaaS's ZATCA submission layer.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Merchant-facing dashboard + acquirer reconciliation for brick-and-mortar branches. Fonoa is an enterprise API for large digital platforms (Uber, Netflix, Booking) — not POS operators.

## TIER 4 — Adjacent pattern / vertical analogs

Useful to know but not direct threats. These prove the *shape* of the play (orchestrate fragmented infrastructure for a regulated industry, sell as SaaS) — not the same product.

### 10. Choice — **★★★**
- **URL:** [choiceqr.com](https://choiceqr.com) · **HQ:** Prague, Czech Republic
- **What:** All-in-one ordering/payments/reservations OS for restaurants. 1.5M monthly orders across 9 CEE markets.
- **Funding:** **$7.1M Series A, March 2026** (total $11.6M). [Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/03/16/choice-secures-71m-series-a-to-expand-its-restaurant-operating-system-across-europe/)
- **Overlap:** Consolidates payments + ops for multi-branch F&B across multiple markets.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Deep reconciliation/acquirer-settlement workflow and ZATCA filing. Choice is guest-side/ordering-first.

### 11. Chift — **★★★**
- **URL:** [chift.eu](https://chift.eu) · **HQ:** Brussels, Belgium
- **What:** Unified API connecting accounting, POS, e-commerce, and invoicing systems for EU SaaS vendors. "Plaid for back-office."
- **Funding:** **€2.3M seed, June 2024**, led by Entourage + Shapers. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/06/25/chift-lets-saas-companies-integrate-with-dozens-of-financial-tools-with-a-unified-api/)
- **Overlap:** Same "stitch POS + accounting + invoicing" plumbing problem across fragmented EU systems.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** End-merchant dashboard with live ZATCA filing. Chift sells infra to SaaS builders, not to merchants directly.

### 12. Akurateco — **★★★**
- **URL:** [akurateco.com](https://akurateco.com) · **HQ:** Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **What:** White-label PCI-DSS payment gateway + orchestration with 200+ connectors. Sold to PSPs, banks, and ISVs.
- **Funding:** **~€300K seed, August 2022**, from Startup Wise Guys. [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/akurateco)
- **Overlap:** Closest peer for the MSP/POS-vendor distribution motion NeoPaaS plays.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Akurateco is generic and geo-agnostic. NeoPaaS bundles pre-integrated Saudi acquirers, ZATCA, and POS fleet monitoring a white-label buyer would otherwise build.

### 13. Stedi — **★★★** *(template, not competitor)*
- **URL:** [stedi.com](https://www.stedi.com) · **HQ:** New York, NY
- **What:** AI-enabled programmable healthcare clearinghouse for eligibility checks and claims. EDI/clearinghouse playbook applied to US healthcare.
- **Funding:** **$50M Series C, 2026**, led by Addition (Stripe, Ribbit, USV); $142M total. [Stedi blog](https://www.stedi.com/blog/series-c)
- **Overlap:** Same structural play — sit between merchants/providers and a fragmented regulated counterparty, automate the data plumbing, sell as SaaS.
- **NeoPaaS wins on:** Different vertical entirely. Use Stedi as the **template/analog for investor framing**, not a head-to-head competitor.

## Strategic takeaway — the moat in one paragraph

The real competitive set is 7 names (Tier 1 + Tier 2 orchestration leaders). Among them:

- **Only one** (Yuno) has any MENA presence.
- **None** have ZATCA Phase 2 e-invoicing built in.
- **None** combine acquirer-level reconciliation with terminal fleet monitoring out of the box.
- **None** distribute through MSPs and POS vendors as the primary channel.
- **None** are pre-integrated with Mada, NeoLeap, Geidea, or NmoHub.

NeoPaaS's defensible position is the **specific bundle** — orchestration + reconciliation + ZATCA + POS fleet monitoring + Saudi-acquirer pre-integration, sold through MSPs and POS vendors. Each capability exists somewhere in the competitor set, but the bundle exists nowhere outside Saudi.

For investor positioning, the cleanest one-line is: *"Yuno is doing what we're doing, just without the Saudi-specific bundle that wins this market."* For sales positioning to merchants, it's: *"Primer doesn't sell to multi-branch chains. Pennylane doesn't reconcile across acquirers. Fonoa doesn't run your terminal fleet. We do all of it, here."*

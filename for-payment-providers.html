Questions partners actually ask

The honest answers.

Why not just build it ourselves?

You could. It's roughly SAR 1.1–3M, 12–18 months, three engineers, and 2.5 FTE of ongoing maintenance per year, forever. Or distribute NeoPaaS, earn 20% on every merchant terminal, and put those three engineers on something that actually differentiates your product.

20% feels high. Why not 10%?

Because we earn it. Build cost is roughly 10x our annual rev share at scale. We hit your break-even in month 2 or 3 — after that, every merchant is pure margin to you, with zero engineering hours billed against it. The 20% is the price of never seeing an integration ticket again.

What if you go down?

99.99% uptime SLA — 4.4 minutes per month maximum, with credit-backed guarantees. We're on Datadog and PagerDuty 24/7. Beyond the SLA: our revenue depends on yours, so our incentive to stay up is structural, not contractual.

Do our merchants know it's NeoPaaS?

Your call. Co-branded (most common), invisible white-label (the merchant only sees your brand), or full-NeoPaaS-branded (least common — usually only when partners want to leverage our compliance story). We support all three.

How does the merchant experience change?

From their side: onboarding goes from weeks to minutes, reconciliation goes from spreadsheets to a real-time dashboard, ZATCA goes from a quarterly nightmare to invisible, and they get one place to see every terminal and every transaction. They'll thank you for it.

What's the technical lift on our side?

Realistically: one engineer, two to three weeks. One API integration to our platform, a webhook for status updates, and a partner portal embed if you want one. We have a sandbox you can hit today. Public docs at docs.neopaas.com.

When do we get paid?

Monthly, on the 5th, for the previous month's revenue. Wired to whatever account you specify. Statements detail revenue per merchant, per terminal, per transaction class. No invoicing required from you.

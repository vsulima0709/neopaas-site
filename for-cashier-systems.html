Questions partners actually ask.

Why not build it ourselves?

You could. It's roughly SAR 2M+, eighteen months, three engineers full-time, and 2.5 FTE of ongoing maintenance per year, forever. Plus you have to keep up with every ZATCA, SAMA, and PCI rule change. Or you embed NeoPaaS, take 25–30% of every subscription, and put those three engineers on what makes your POS actually different.

25–30% feels high. Why not 10%?

Because we earn it. The build alternative costs 10x our annual rev share at scale. We hit your break-even in month 2 or 3 — after that, every customer is pure margin to you, with zero engineering hours billed against it. The 25–30% is the price of never seeing an integration ticket again.

How does white-label actually work?

Three options. (1) Invisible — your customers see only your brand; NeoPaaS isn't mentioned anywhere. (2) Co-branded — "Payments powered by NeoPaaS" small text in the footer. (3) Powered by — explicit attribution. Most partners pick option 1. Switching between is a config change, not a code change.

What does the integration look like on our side?

Realistically: one engineer, two to three weeks. One API integration to our platform, a webhook for status updates, and an embedded merchant dashboard if you want one (we provide a React component). Public docs at docs.neopaas.com. Sandbox available today.

What if you go down?

99.99% uptime SLA — 4.4 minutes per month maximum, with credit-backed guarantees. Datadog and PagerDuty 24/7. Beyond the SLA: our revenue depends on yours, so our incentive to stay up is structural. We've had zero customer-impacting outages in 2025.

When do we get paid?

Monthly, on the 5th, for the previous month. Wire to whatever account you specify. Statement details revenue per customer, per terminal, per transaction class. No invoicing required from you.

Can we sell to customers outside our existing book?

Yes. The rev share applies to any customer that lands on NeoPaaS through your product. We don't restrict your sales motion — go after new logos the same way you do today, just now with unified payments included.
